New Relationship Energy Polyamory

As a polyamorous person, I've often found myself caught up in the excitement of new relationships. The thrill of meeting someone new, the rush of falling for them, and the joy of exploring what our relationship could be – it's intoxicating. But as much as we might try to slow down and savor these feelings, it's hard not to get swept away by the sheer energy of it all. In this post, I want to talk about what I call "new relationship energy polyamory" – the way that our excitement and enthusiasm can both energize and overwhelm our existing relationships.

What is New Relationship Energy Polyamory?

New relationship energy (NRE) is a term used in polyamorous communities to describe the intense, all-consuming feelings we get when we meet someone new. It's like a spark has been lit, and everything feels fresh and exciting. But NRE can be both a blessing and a curse. On one hand, it brings us closer to our new partner, making communication, intimacy, and shared activities feel effortless. On the other hand, NRE can lead us to neglect or overlook existing relationships in favor of the new ones.

Navigating NRE in Polyamorous Relationships

So how do we navigate this energy when it comes to our existing relationships? One key strategy is communication. When you're feeling swept up in NRE, take a step back and talk to your partner(s) about what's going on. Share your feelings, but also acknowledge the impact of your new relationship on others. This might mean having regular check-ins with each other to ensure that everyone's needs are being met.

Another strategy is boundary-setting. Just because you're excited about someone new doesn't mean you can start dropping everything for them or ignoring existing commitments. Set clear boundaries around what you can and can't do in your new relationship, especially if it conflicts with other responsibilities or relationships.

The Impact of NRE on Existing Relationships

NRE can have a significant impact on our existing relationships – both positive and negative. On the positive side, meeting someone new can reignite passion and excitement in an existing relationship. It might prompt us to try new things, be more adventurous, or prioritize quality time together.

However, NRE can also lead to feelings of jealousy, insecurity, or resentment in our existing relationships. If we start prioritizing the new partner over our existing one, it can feel like a betrayal or neglect. To mitigate this, make an effort to stay connected with your existing partner(s) – schedule regular dates, check in regularly, and prioritize shared activities.

Practicing Polyamory with NRE

Polyamory is all about managing multiple relationships simultaneously, and navigating NRE requires a mix of creativity, communication, and self-awareness. Here are some practical tips for practicing polyamory while keeping your new relationship energy in check:

* Prioritize existing commitments and responsibilities. * Communicate openly with all partners involved. * Set clear boundaries around what you can and can't do. * Schedule regular dates and quality time with each partner.

Conclusion

Navigating new relationship energy in polyamory requires a delicate balance between excitement and responsibility. By communicating openly, setting clear boundaries, and prioritizing existing commitments, we can harness the positive aspects of NRE while minimizing its negative impact on our relationships. Remember that every relationship is unique, so take the time to tailor these strategies to your own needs and circumstances.
