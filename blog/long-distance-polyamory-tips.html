Long Distance Polyamory Tips

As a polyamorous person, I've had my fair share of navigating long distance relationships. And let me tell you, it's not for the faint of heart. But with some intentional communication, creative problem-solving, and a willingness to adapt, you can thrive in a long distance polyamorous relationship. In this post, I'll share some practical tips that have helped me and my partners navigate the ups and downs of long distance polyamory.

1. Establish Clear Communication Channels

We make time for regular video calls (at least once a week) to catch up on each other's lives, discuss any issues or concerns, and plan future visits. We also use messaging apps to stay in touch throughout the day, sharing thoughts, feelings, and experiences. To avoid misunderstandings, we've established clear guidelines for communication, including what times are best for calls, how often to check in, and how to handle conflicts.

2. Set Realistic Expectations

I used to get frustrated when my partner wouldn't be available at exactly the right moment. But I realized that's just not possible with different time zones and schedules. Instead of getting hung up on specific expectations, we focus on making the most of our together time. We plan ahead, scheduling dedicated blocks for video calls or in-person visits. This helps us prioritize quality over quantity and appreciate the time we do have together.

Creating a Shared Online Calendar

We use Google Calendar to keep track of each other's schedules and important dates (birthdays, anniversaries, etc.). By sharing our calendars, we can plan around each other's availability and make sure we're not overcommitting or conflicting. This tool has been a game-changer for us!

3. Foster Emotional Intimacy

We prioritize emotional intimacy by making time for regular check-ins, even when we're apart. We share our feelings, desires, and fears with each other, creating a safe space for vulnerability and connection. To stay connected on an emotional level, we also engage in activities like journaling, meditation, or reading together (even if it's just over video call).

Surprise and Delight

We love surprising each other with small gestures – whether it's a thoughtful gift, a sweet message, or a fun surprise delivery. These tiny acts of kindness help us feel seen and loved from afar, even when we can't be together in person.

4. Make Time for In-Person Visits

We prioritize scheduling regular in-person visits whenever possible. Whether it's a quick weekend getaway or a longer trip, these visits are essential for rekindling our connection and creating shared memories. To make the most of these visits, we plan ahead, booking activities, restaurants, and accommodations that suit everyone's tastes.

Creating a Shared Online Journal

We keep an online journal where we share our thoughts, feelings, and experiences during in-person visits. This helps us reflect on our time together, processing the emotions and memories created during those special moments.

5. Practice Self-Care and Trust

Long distance polyamory can be emotionally draining, so it's essential to prioritize self-care and trust-building. We focus on nurturing our individual relationships with ourselves, engaging in activities that nourish our minds, bodies, and spirits. By trusting each other's intentions and commitments, we create a foundation of security and stability – even when apart.

Long distance polyamory requires effort, patience, and creativity, but with the right mindset and tools, it can be an incredible journey of growth, connection, and love. Remember to prioritize communication, emotional intimacy, and trust-building, and don't be afraid to adapt and evolve as you navigate this unique experience together.
