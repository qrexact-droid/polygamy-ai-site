How To Meet Poly Partners

If you're interested in exploring polyamory or ethical non-monogamy, finding like-minded partners can be a daunting task. With the stigma surrounding non-traditional relationships still prevalent in many communities, it's easy to feel isolated and unsure of where to start looking for potential partners. But fear not! I've been there too, and I'm here to share some practical advice on how to meet poly partners.

Online Communities and Forums

One of the most effective ways to connect with people who share your interests in non-monogamy is through online communities and forums. There are numerous websites, social media groups, and online forums dedicated specifically to polyamory and ethical non-monogamy. Some popular options include:

  • Polyamory.org: A comprehensive resource for all things polyamory, including a community forum where you can connect with others.
  • The Polyamory subreddit: A community of over 100,000 people discussing various aspects of polyamory and non-monogamy.
  • OkCupid's Non-Monogamous Community: OkCupid has a dedicated space for users who identify as non-monogamous or polyamorous to connect with others.

Taking the time to read through online forums, participate in discussions, and engage with others can help you build connections and friendships that may eventually lead to romantic relationships. Be sure to follow each community's rules and guidelines for interacting with others.

Polyamory Meetups and Events

Meetup.com is a great resource for finding local polyamory meetups, events, and groups. These events can range from casual gatherings to workshops and conferences focused on non-monogamy. Attend these events not only to network with others but also to learn more about polyamory and its various practices.

Some popular types of events include:

  • Speed friending: A fun way to meet multiple people in a short amount of time, often used as an icebreaker for larger gatherings.
  • Workshops on communication, boundaries, and jealousy management.
  • Polyamory conferences and retreats: Opportunities to connect with others while learning from experts in the field.

Local Polyamory Groups

Look for local polyamory groups, either through online research or word of mouth. These groups often host regular meetups, events, and activities that can help you build connections with others in your area. Be sure to reach out to these groups directly to learn more about their events and membership requirements.

Poly-Friendly Events

Polyamory is not limited to specific events or gatherings; many general events and festivals can be great places to meet like-minded individuals. Look for events that promote inclusivity, diversity, and open-mindedness, such as:

  • LGBTQ+ Pride events.
  • Alternative lifestyle conventions (e.g., fetish or kink communities).
  • Intimacy and relationship workshops.

Being Open and Honest with Your Interests

It's essential to be open and honest about your interests in polyamory when meeting new people. This can be daunting, especially if you're concerned about rejection or judgment. However, honesty is key in building meaningful connections.

  • Be clear about what you're looking for: whether it's a casual friendship, a romantic connection, or something more serious.
  • Share your experiences and thoughts on polyamory to help others understand where you're coming from.
  • Don't be afraid to ask questions and learn from others' perspectives.

Conclusion

Meeting poly partners requires patience, persistence, and an open mind. By exploring online communities, attending local events, and being honest about your interests, you can increase your chances of connecting with like-minded individuals. Remember that building relationships takes time, and it's essential to prioritize respect, communication, and mutual understanding.

I hope this advice has been helpful in your search for poly partners. Keep an open heart and mind, and don't be discouraged by setbacks or rejections – every connection you make brings you closer to finding the right people with whom to explore the world of non-monogamy.
